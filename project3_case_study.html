Full Case Study: A Data-Driven Strategy for Cyclistic Bike-Share

Introduction

This full case study dives deeper into the methodology, data processing, statistical analysis, and visualization that led to actionable recommendations for Cyclistic’s marketing team.

Objective

The main goal was to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently, and identify strategies to convert casual riders into loyal members.

Data Collection

We used real Divvy datasets from Q1 2019 and Q1 2020, containing trip-level data with ride duration, start/end stations, and rider type.

Data Cleaning Process

  • Removed duplicate records
  • Filtered out rides with negative or unrealistic durations
  • Standardized column formats and names
  • Verified data integrity across multiple sources

Analysis

Using R, we calculated descriptive statistics, plotted usage patterns, and performed statistical tests to validate observed differences.

Key Findings

  • Casual riders take longer trips, especially on weekends
  • Annual members ride more frequently during weekdays
  • Peak usage times differ significantly between groups

Recommendations

  • Offer weekend promotions to casual riders
  • Create trial memberships targeting weekend users
  • Launch behavior-based email marketing campaigns

Conclusion

This analysis provided Cyclistic’s marketing team with clear, data-backed strategies to increase membership conversions, highlighting the value of integrating analytics into business decision-making.
